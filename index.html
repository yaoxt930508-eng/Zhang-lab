Designing precision phage cocktails through interaction-guided discovery

We investigate phage–bacteria interactions, host-range architecture, co-infection compatibility, and rational phage cocktail design for multidrug-resistant bacterial infections.

Research

From interaction matrices to optimized phage therapeutics

01

Phage–host interaction mapping

We construct large-scale lysis matrices to quantify phage activity across diverse bacterial strain collections.

02

Compatibility-aware cocktail design

Co-infection interaction data are used to exclude antagonistic phage pairs and prioritize compatible combinations.

03

Experimental validation

Optimized cocktails are tested against clinical isolates over extended time courses to evaluate inhibition breadth and durability.

Featured figure

Matrix-guided workflow for phage cocktail optimization

Figure. Exhaustive generation of candidate phage cocktails from lysis and co-infection interaction matrices. Antagonistic combinations are removed, and remaining cocktails are ranked using coverage breadth, coverage redundancy, and taxonomic diversity. Selected cocktails are validated against an expanded panel of Enterococcus faecium strains.
